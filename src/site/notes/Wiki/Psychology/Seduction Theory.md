of [[People/Sigmund Freud\|Sigmund Freud]]

Freud's "seduction theory" from the mid-[[Calendar/Decades/1890s\|1890s]] proposed that hysteria and obsessional neurosis came from hidden memories of early childhood sexual abuse. Initially, Freud believed his patients were recounting real experiences of abuse, thinking such events caused many of their mental health issues. However, he soon changed his view, concluding that these memories were more likely imagined fantasies, not actual events.
